About

Large language models (LLMs) have demonstrated unprecedented capabilities that present both transformative opportunities and significant challenges for recommender systems. Our workshop explores the bidirectional synergy between LLMs and recommender systems through two complementary directions: LLM4Rec, which focuses on leveraging LLMs' reasoning, semantic understanding, and generative capabilities to enhance recommendation quality and user experience; and Rec4LLM, which investigates how recommendation techniques and rich user-item interaction data can advance LLM development and deployment. We bring together leading researchers and practitioners from academia and industry to address critical challenges including cold-start scenarios, explainability, interactive paradigms, and scaling laws, while exploring novel applications such as reasoning-enhanced recommendations, AI-generated content, and LLM agent coordination. Through keynote presentations, interactive panels, and collaborative discussions, our workshop aims to establish unified understanding and identify promising research directions at this rapidly evolving intersection, ultimately shaping the future of next-generation recommender systems in the LLM era.
